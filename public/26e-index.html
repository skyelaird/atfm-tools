26E CTP — Post-Performance Analyses

VATCAN eastbound 2026-04-25 · 1163 booked flights · CZQM/CZQX entries
A working set of analyses built around the 26E NAT-eastbound CTP event. Starts with the predicted sector-load model (the input the CTP team works from), layers a per-flight wind-uncertainty lens at three forecast lead times, extends the same model to airport-level arrival demand at LFPG, captures live observation during the event, and closes with a post-event actual-vs-predicted comparison. Together they're a proof-of-concept for the demand-modelling pipeline we'd want for Westbound CTP 2027.

Key findings

  • Wind error is small relative to launch-pattern variance. Pilot launch slip + filing variance dominates total ETA uncertainty (~89%); wind contributes ~11% of T-72 variance. Even perfect wind forecasting reduces total uncertainty by only ~6%.
  • Per-flight wind uncertainty scales with flight time (shift = t_to_point × X / GS). At T-72 (X=17 kt), a sector crossed at 30 min shifts ±1 min; at 4 hours, ±10 min. The same booked traffic appears tighter at near-origin sectors and wider at OXP / arrival.
  • At the fleet-aggregate scale, wind uncertainty cancels by √N. Per-flight residual of 4 min RMSE × ~16 flights/hr/track → ±1 aircraft/hr at 60-min bins, ±1.5 at 15-min. Sub-15-min bins cross into PBCS-margin territory (5-min spacing absorbs ±4 min residuals by design).
  • Capacity, not arrival demand, sets the operational stage. LFPG can absorb 60-70/hr — destination AAR almost never binds. Track count × PBCS spacing (12-20/hr/track), origin departure throughput, and downstream-sector limits are what CTOT allocation must respect. Wind-uncertainty bands at the demand-curve level are a sanity check, not a planning driver.
  • Mach + GRIB at T-72 is operationally trustworthy for track-throughput planning. It absorbs ~85-90% of the day's wind bias and leaves residual <1-2 aircraft/hr at OXP-distance hourly bins. Switching from static-TAS to T-72 GRIB integration is roughly a 5× improvement in hourly demand-forecast error, from "track-decision-relevant" to "noise."
